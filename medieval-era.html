Medieval Era • 12th Century CE

Pattachitra: Painted Devotion

Divine narratives in vibrant pigments

Sacred Scroll Paintings

Painted during Lord Jagannath's Rath Yatra, Pattachitra scrolls bloom with natural pigments and epic tales. Every stroke whispers devotion, preserving Odisha's rich temple traditions.

These vibrant masterpieces weave together divine narratives, transforming cloth and palm leaves into portable shrines that carry the essence of ancient temples into homes and hearts.

🎨

Natural Pigments

Traditional colors derived from minerals, plants, and conch shells create the distinctive palette that has remained unchanged for centuries.

📜

Epic Narratives

Stories from Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Jagannath legends come alive through intricate visual storytelling and symbolic representations.

🏛️

Temple Traditions

Deeply connected to Jagannath Temple rituals, these paintings serve as devotional objects during Rath Yatra and other sacred festivals.
